A critical scalability bottleneck

A critical operational process was challenged when handling a sudden surge in client requests triggered by shifting market conditions. Because legacy systems and fragmented processes could not support the increased volume, the business faced a persistent inability to complete requests on time and a high rate of data issues. Business stakeholders turned to our strategic platform for help.

Manual complexity and high latency

18 manual touch points and 10 manual data transfers between teams. The end-to-end process typically took 14 days, involving 4 separate teams and 9 different tools.

Nuance in nature

The process is full of nuance. Reflecting and validating complex results is a high-stakes, manual ordeal.

Data quality suffered

System not having proper rules governing actions meant data errors were only discovered after committed. It caused long data reconciliation work, leading to high-stakes regulatory and reputation risk.

The Strategy: Bring one team in earlier

I led the fundamental rethinking of how these cross-functional teams interacted, moving beyond interface design to redesign the core operational workflow.

I used service blueprint to identify a critical timing risk, leading to an architectural reversal where one team was brought in earlier.

Enabled automation

Capturing governing rules and data upfront enabled downstream processing automation

Efficiency unlocked

The reversed order shifted team's priority, which turned a process of 14 days into a one-day timely completion

0 manual data reconciliations

Front-loaded real time data validation in one system eliminated human errors and after-match data reconciliation

User instructed automation

Let users give instructions to system automation to eliminate manual labor while covering nuance cases

Guided experience in one tool

Unified a convoluted process into a guided experience in one workflow, removing the needs for tooling and workarounds.
