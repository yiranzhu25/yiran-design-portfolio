Project context

Onboarding a loan — Read the rulebook before playing the game

Once Goldman Sachs closes a loan, our internal teams start onboard and manage the loan. The first thing to onboard a loan is to take all financial terms in the loan contract and digitize them into a system. This contract is the rulebook for how the teams should manage the loan.

The process has two steps:

Step 1: A vendor extract financial terms from raw documents
Step 2: Our loan team review all terms for accuracy

The challenges we face are:

Vendor dependency

Our platform now is a container to display these financial terms but has no intelligence to extract financial terms. We rely on vendor which is slow, expensive and error-prone

Manual review

The review team had to manually cross-check hundreds of terms against raw legal documents, running 10 days late because of this labor intense process and sheer volume of loans.

Problem space

Locating hundreds of rules in the hundred-page-long rulebook is difficult

I facilitated user sessions to identify the key efficiency and risk gaps and gathered metrics to understand the size of the problem.

Key insights:

  • Biggest efficiency gap

    Reviewers spent 60% of their time just locating the right terms in hundred-page contracts.

  • Biggest risk gap

    No error prevention or system checks for users to rely on.

  • A new concern

    How would users trust that AI interpreted terms correctly?

As the design owner, I owned end-to-end design of two interconnected challenges — replacing the vendor with AI extraction, and redesigning the human review experience around it.

Challenge 1: Introducing AI extraction

Core design questions:

  • How to surface AI output?
  • What does it look like when AI is confident vs. uncertain?
  • How does AI hand off to a human reviewer in a way that builds trust?

I experimented two directions, and landed on Direction 2 for its strategic fit for other use cases across our platform.

Challenge 2: Human review experience

Core design questions:

  • How do we eliminate the biggest pain — scrolling through hundreds of pages to find one source?
  • What tools support reviewers without replacing their judgment?
  • How can reviewers override AI output easily and move through review fast without sacrificing accuracy?
